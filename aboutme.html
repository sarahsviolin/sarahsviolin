About

Sarah started violin at the age of seven and had a humble musical education. After joining the community youth symphony at 12 years old, she discovered violin to be a passionate hobby that will always be a part of her life. Sarah started studying with Walter Schwede, professor at Western Washington University and concert master of Seattle Symphony, who helped her advance tremendously with solo repertoire and chamber ensemble work. During the first year of her studies with Professor Schwede, Sarah won the regional Solo and Ensemble Contest in solo violin and small ensemble category. She later went to represent her high school in the state competition. Sarah continued her education with Lawrence Sonderling, a former violinist at the Los Angeles Philharmonic and graduated from Occidental College, with a degree in music performance.She started teaching in 2012 to young children and and older adults, hoping to spread the importance of hard work, self expression, and beautiful music.
